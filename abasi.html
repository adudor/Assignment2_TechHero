Dr. Abasi Ene-Obong

Early Life and Education

Dr. Abasi Ene-Obong is a native of Nigeria and has a masters' in buisness and management from Claremont Colleges, a masters's in human molecular genetics from Imperial College London, while also holding a PhD in Cancer Biology from University of London. Ene-Obong has worked in the US, UK and Nigerian healthcare systems doing his research and furthering his company.

Professional Career and Accolades

Dr. Abasi Ene-Obong is the Founder and CEO of 54gene, a health technology company that focuses on utilizing the African genome to fuel genetic research. 54gene works to collect and analyze genetic data that can advance discovery and relevant drug development. Ene-Obong and his company have built strong relationships with researchers all over Africa, with his company being able to biobank tens of thousands of African genomes. Founded in 2019, 54gene has made the Time Magazine list of 12 innovations that would change healthcare in the 2020s and has been listed by Nigeria’s Punch newspaper as 1 of 5 innovations that would improve healthcare for Africans. 54gene also made the Fast Company list of the world’s ‘Most Innovative Companies’ in 2020.
